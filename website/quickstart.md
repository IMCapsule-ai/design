# Quickstart — 90 seconds

Three steps to your first design.

## 1. Download &amp; install

Pick the installer for your platform from the [Download section](/#download), then:

- **macOS** — open the `.dmg`, drag **Palette Design** into `/Applications`, double-click to launch.
- **Windows** — run the `.exe` setup, follow the installer prompts.
- **Linux** — `chmod +x palette-design-*.AppImage` then double-click, or install the `.deb`.

Palette launches into the welcome screen and seeds a starter workspace at `~/Documents/Palette`.

## 2. Type your first prompt

The app opens on the **Designs hub**. Pick a built-in template — *Landing page*, *Dashboard*, *Pitch deck*, *Pricing page*, *Mobile app*, *Chat UI*, *Calendar*, *Blog article*, *Receipt*, *Portfolio*, *Email* — or hit **New design** and describe what you want.

Examples:

> A pricing page for a developer-tools startup. Three tiers: Hobby (free), Pro ($29/mo), Team ($99/mo). Use a soft indigo palette with rounded cards.

> A dashboard for a small e-commerce store. Show today's revenue, top 5 products, conversion rate, and recent orders.

> A landing page for a meditation app. Calm, editorial typography, soft greens, hero CTA "Start your 7-day trial."

The first design renders in seconds in a sandboxed preview. You'll see hover states, tabs, and empty states already wired up.

## 3. Refine, export, hand off

Three ways to refine without re-prompting the whole page:

- **Comment mode** — click any element, drop a pin, leave a note. Palette rewrites just that region.
- **AI sliders** — Palette emits the parameters worth tweaking (color, spacing, font size). Drag to refine.
- **Component sidebar** — open any block in isolation, edit its tokens, see the preview update live.

When you're done, hit **Export**:

- **HTML** — single-file, inlined CSS, ready to paste anywhere.
- **PDF** — print-quality export for handoff.
- **PPTX** — fully editable PowerPoint deck.
- **ZIP** — multi-file source bundle.
- **Markdown** — for spec docs and READMEs.

All exports happen on-device. No cloud round-trip.
